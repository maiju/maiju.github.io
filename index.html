Improving Project Management System in R&D with Human-Centered Design

Master's thesis

The thesis aimed to understand project managers, their needs and usage of a project management system. The objective was to provide improvement suggestions of how the system could become more useful for the project managers.

Human-centered design methods (contextual inquiry, questionnaire, idea workshops) were used to contribute to the understanding of users and their context of use, and the creation of supportive design solutions.

The thesis was conducted for Kemira's R&D organization from May 2013 to Jan 2014.

tulevala

Concept created on course Information networks: Studio X

The concept we created is aimed for high-school aged students and is eventually a career simulation game.

The concept aims to help students in choosing their future study places and careers in a career simulation game which imitates real life work conditions. The concept was created to tackle the uncertainty of students and lack of knowledge of for example what does a M.Sc. (tech) or MBA really do. On society level, the benefit would be on reducing gap years and study place changes.The office simulation provides real experiences of the environment, work tasks and team work of a specific topic or type of work. The concept focused on abstract academic jobs but could basically be expanded to any field.

During concept development we were engaged in brainstorming, facilitated a prototype game, and pitched our concept in a presentation.

tuntuQ

Concept created on course User Inspired Design, Aalto ARTS

The initial starting point for our concept was a question of "How to improve well-being through the sense of touch?"

We created a product concept which is based on providing a set of different materials to stimulate the sense of touch. Half-moon shaped pieces are all compatible with each other and provide different kinds of touching sensations especially for feet.

During the design proccess, we used several idea creation methods, conducted expert interviews, created design probes and personas, and validated concept with users.

Fling a bug

Game created during course Information Networks: Studio 4

The topic of the course was to give an introduction to computer graphics through creative group work in Processing environment. In one of the assignments we explored different kinds of interaction techniques.

Our game, Fling a bug, is interacted through web camera and microphone. The idea to protect a leaf from being eaten by bugs. Waving playmark in hand and using “shout tool” will fling the bugs away from the leaf.
